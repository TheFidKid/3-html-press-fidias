Mental Health

Teenage Depression

♦Approximately 20% of teens experience depression before they reach adulthood

♦Female Teenagers develop depression twice as often as male teens

♦Only 30% of teens with depression are receiving treatment

♦Every 100 minutes a teen takes their own life

♦Suicide is the third-leading cause of death for those between the ages of 15 and 24

What is Depression?

Symptoms of Depression

♦Feelings of sadness, worthlessness or lack of hope

♦Loss of interest in once enjoyed activities

♦Changes in appetite

♦Too little or too much sleep

♦Loss of energy

♦Increased purposeless physical activity (e.g. pacing)

♦Difficulty thinking, concentrating or decision making

♦Thoughts of death or suicide

How it can be treated

There are many forms of treatment that are available for depression today. There are many medications, ranging from SSRI, antidepressants, anxiolytic and antipsychotic medication. If depression is severe enough, ECT is a viable medical procedure which safely stimulates a seizure and temporarily cures symptoms. There are also therapists, who can help. The possible therapies range from cognitive behavioral therapy, behavior therapy and psychotherapy. There are many options available and because so many teens go untreated, an effort should be made to make teenagers aware of this.

Anxiety and its effects

Anxiety

Anxiety disorder is a disorder where the person experiences feelings of worry, anxiety and/or fear that interfere with everyday life.

Symptoms

Fatigued body, restlessness and sweating

Lack of focus, mind swarmed with thoughts

Excessive worry, feara, insomnia, nausea, palpitations, trembling

Treatment

Self-Care

Avoid of caffeine

Exercise and keep a healthy diet

Medication

SSRI, Anxiolytic, Antidepressant, Sedative, and Nerve Pain medication

Therapy

Cognitive behavioral therapy, Meditation and Psychotherapy
